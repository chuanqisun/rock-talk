## Theme: Creativity for Change

Submissions should explore how creativity and its processes can:

- inspire and enable societal transformation,
- drive positive change,
- address decolonization,
- tackle climate action,
- enquire into environmental and posthumanist relationships,
- support place-making in urban and cultural contexts.

Works should highlight how interactive systems, design, and creative processes/outputs can foster positive change and respond to global challenges.

**Note:** If your submission was accepted to C&C25, it must be resubmitted for consideration for C&C26 Arts gallery space in London (UK).

## Call Description (What Can Be Submitted)

The exhibition invites works such as:

- physical-digital objects (e.g., generative artworks),
- photography,
- animation, film, and moving image,
- sound pieces,
- interactive installations,
- virtual / augmented / mixed reality,
- performances,
- works suited to special technological platforms.

In your application, indicate **spatial, technical, and environmental needs**, such as:

- power and/or wired/Wi‑Fi internet,
- lighting conditions (dark/spot-lit),
- audio needs (listening/sound output),
- indoor/outdoor suitability,
- duration and run mode (once/looping), etc.

Audiences may include conference attendees, university staff and students, and invited guests. Works should be able to adapt as needed to exhibition space requirements and available equipment.

## Submission Format & Templates

- Prepare artwork papers using the [ACM submission template](https://authors.acm.org/proceedings/production-information) (LaTeX/Word available) in **single-column** format.
- The **Project Description** must be submitted as a **PDF** in the ACM SIGCHI standardized **single-column** format (as it will be included in the curated proceedings).

## What to Submit (2 Documents)

Submit **two** documents:

### (1) Project Description (PDF)

- **Max length:** 2000 words (excluding titles, references, and figure/table captions)
- Must include:
  - Narrative description of the piece (conceptual goals and functional operations)
  - Links to documentation and/or visual portfolio

### (2) Technical Rider

Specify technical/logistical requirements, including:

- work dimensions and space requirements,
- equipment requirements,
- installation requirements for software and/or hardware,
- scheduling requirements for real-time works (if applicable),
- shipping needs to/from gallery space (if applicable),
- images/schemas (optional),
- **safety implications** (e.g., soldering) and any health & safety compliance needs,
- reference exhibition space: atrium space called **[The Street](https://www.arts.ac.uk/__data/assets/pdf_file/0029/496037/CSM-Innnovation-CE-and-VH-Brochure-Digital.pdf)** inside Central Saint Martins.

## Review Model

- **Single-blind peer reviewed**; authors **do not need to anonymize** submissions.
- Submissions are juried by the C&C 26 Curatorial Advisory Committee.

## Acceptance, Attendance, and Exhibition Expectations

- Accepted artworks are included in the proceedings and distributed via the conference and the ACM Digital Library.
- Authors are expected to **register and attend in person**, be available to **install/de-rig**, and participate in the exhibition opening and presentation to attendees.
